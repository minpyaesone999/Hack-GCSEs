Pollution

  • Discuss the causes of acid rain.
      • fossil fuels contain sulfur
      • sulfur is burnt and sulfur dioxide is released
      • nitrogen reacts with oxygen in vehicle exhausts to from nitrogen oxide
      • these acidic gases react with water in clouds to form acid rain
  • Discuss the negative effects of acid rain on land.
      • damages plants and trees
      • soil leaching
      • nutrients in soil no longer available to plants
      • dissolves limestone
      • damages buildings and statues
  • Describe the negative effects of acid rain on freshwater ecosystems, such as rivers, streams, and lakes.
      • pH of lakes and rivers decrease
      • nutrients like nitrate ions are leached
      • shells are damaged
      • fish fail to reproduce
      • aquatic plants die
      • disrupts food chains (describe more)
      • acidic environment kills aquatic animals
  • State the measures that are taken to reduce sulfur dioxide pollution and reduce the impact of acid rain.
      • scrubbers in chimneys of power stations → neutralize waste gases with lime
      • desulfurization of coal
      • use less fossil fuels
      • use alternative sources of energy like solar power
      • use catalytic converters in cars

  • Describe the effects of waterborne chemical wastes on the environment.
      • heavy metals are toxin to organisms
      • bioaccumulation of heavy metal → passed along the food chain and accumulates to organisms with high trophic levels
      • decreases pH and acid burns shells/skin/plants
  • Describe and explain what happens when eutrophication occurs.
      • fertilizer leeches into river/stream/lake
      • increased availability of nitrate and other ions
      • algae bloom
      • algae block sunlight from entering water
      • death of other producers after being unable to photosynthesize
      • increased decomposition
      • increased aerobic respiration
      • decomposers use up oxygen in the water
      • organisms like fish die due to lack of oxygen
  • How can we reduce the chances of eutrophication occurring when applying fertilizers?
      • calculate accurately how much fertilizer is needed and use only the amount that is needed
      • do not apply fertilizers during or after rain
      • make channels between land and water body
      • only apply when crops are growing in the area
  • Discuss the effects of non-biodegradable plastics in the environment, in terrestrial ecosystems.
      • blocks digestive systems of animals
      • ref. to toxins and chemical fumes
      • bioaccummulation → passes down the food chain
      • habitat destruction → plastic covers habitats
      • blocks photosynthesis for land plants
      • blocks root growth
      • remain in the ecosystem for a long time
  • Discuss the effects of non-biodegradable plastics in the environment, in aquatic ecosystems.
      • plastic remains for a long time (not easily decomposed)
      • ingested by organisms but not digested → blocks gut
      • entangled and cut by plastic
      • plastic blocks sunlight for photosynthesis
      • may release toxins and harmful chemicals
      • blocks the flow of water in streams or rivers
      • reduces concentration of dissolved oxygen
      • bioaccumulation
  • Explain how carbon dioxide enhances the greenhouse effect.
      • light from the Sun hits Earth
      • short-wave radiation passes through carbon dioxide layer
      • reflected form ground as long-wave radiation → infrared
      • this infrared wave is heat energy, and it's trapped and prevented from escaping from atmosphere by carbon dioxide
  • Describe the negative impacts of female contraceptive hormones in water courses.
      • reduced sperm count in men
      • feminization of aquatic organisms

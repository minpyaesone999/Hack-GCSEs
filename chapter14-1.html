Nervous control in humans

  • Define nerve impulse.
      • an electrical signal
      • that passes along nerve cells called neurones

  • Describe how the structure of a neurone is related to its function.
      • it's long to transmit impulses over a long distance faster
      • it has mitochondria because impulse transmission require energy
      • it contains vesicles to contain neurotransmitters into the synapse
      • receptors allow unidirectional transmission

  • Define reflex action.
      • a means of automatically and rapidly
      • integrating and coordinating stimuli
      • with the responses of effectors (muscles and glands)
  • Why do reflexes still occur in people who are unconscious?
      • reflexes are involuntary
      • neurones still function
  • Describe the differences between voluntary action and involuntary action.
      • voluntary action takes more time
      • voluntary action has conscious control
      • voluntary action is learnt
      • voluntary action is not automatic
      • voluntary action response is not always same to the stimulus
  • Define synapse.
      • junction between two neurones
  • Describe how impulses pass from one neurone to another neurone across a synapse.
      • vesicles containing neurotransmitters move to the cell membrane
      • vesicles fuse with cell membrane
      • neurotransmitters are released from vesicles into the synapse
      • neurotransmitters diffuse across synapse
      • bind with receptor molecules on neurone surface on the other side of synapse
      • neurotransmitter and receptor are complementary
      • this causes impulse to continue
  • Suggest how the structure of a synapse ensure that impulses travel in one direction.
      • neurotransmitters are released on one side of synapse
      • receptors are only found on the other side of the synapse

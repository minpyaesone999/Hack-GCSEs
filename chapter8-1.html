Water uptake

  • State the functions of xylem and phloem.
      • xylem: transports water
      • phloem: transports sucrose and amino acids
  • Identify the position of xylem and phloem in roots, stems, and leaves.
      • xylem: inner in root and stem; upper in leaves
      • phloem: outer in root and stem, lower in leaves

  • Describe the pathway of water from outside the root to the xylem vessels. || Outline how water that has entered a root hair cell reaches the stomata. || Explain how the loss of water from the leaves helps to move more water from the roots to the leaves.
      • active transport of ions to create a water potential gradient
      • the soil now has a higher water potential than the root hair cells
      • water moves from an area of higher water potential to lower water potential
      • water enters root hair cells via osmosis
      • through a partially permeable membrane
      • root hair cell - root cortex - xylem vessles
      • cohesion of water molecules and adhesion of water to xylem pulls on a whole water column in xylem
      • water moves up the xylem
      • water moves into leaf by osmosis
      • transpiration occurs, water diffuses out of stomata
      • reduced water potential at top of the plant creates another water potential gradient which draws more columns of water up the xylem continuously
      • this is called transpiration pull
  • Describe how the xylem is adapted for its function.
      • no cytoplasm - they're hollow
      • they have walls made of lignin - strong walls
      • walls are waterproof
      • long elongated cells
      • bordered pits for water movement between vessels
  • Suggest why there is little difference in the water flow-rate in healthy roots and in roots treated with the toxic solution.
      • xylem vessels are dead so toxins have no effect
      • osmosis water flow into root - does not rely on living cells
      • no need for energy, it's a passive mode of transport

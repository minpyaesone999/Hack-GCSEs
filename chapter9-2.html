Coronary Heart Disease

  • Describe what coronary heart dissease is and state the possible risk factors.
      • it is the blockage of coronary arteries
      • risk factors: fatty det, stress, smoking, genetic predisposition, age, gender
  • Past-Paper Question 1
    • from: 0610/41/O/N/19
  • Describe how too much fat in the diet may cause coronary heart disease.
      • fat is deposited in walls of coronary arteries
      • blood flow is restricted in arteries
      • no blood flow to cardiac muscles
      • less nutrients (glucose and oxygen) reach heart muscles
  • Describe the effect on the heart of a blockage in the coronary artery.
      • lack of blood supply to heart muscles
      • insufficient oxygen or glucose
      • less aerobic respiration
      • cardiac cells die
      • heart muscle cannot contract
  • Explain why exercise is recommended for people with high risk of developing coronary heart disease.
      • prevents blocked arteries
      • lowers blood pressure
      • lowers cholesterol
      • fat loss
      • lowers stress
      • stronger heart muscles
  • Describe and explain how coronary heart disease may be treated.
    • Drug treatment
      • take aspirin
      • to reduce the clotting of blood
    • Surgery
      • coronary artery by-pass graft:
      • a piece of blood vessel from thigh vein is attached to carry blood around the blocked artery
      • angioplasty:
      • a balloon is inserted into arery and inflated to widen artery
      • stent:
      • to hold arteries open to restore blood supply to heart muscle

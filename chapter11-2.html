Breathing

  • Describe the mechanism of expiration.
      • internal intercostal muscles contract
      • external intercostal muscles relax
      • pulls ribs inwards
      • diaphragm relaxes and rises
      • volume of thorax/chest/lungs decreases
      • pressure in thorax/chest/lungs increases
      • air flows out of body down the pressure gradient
  • Describe the mechanism of inspiration.
      • external intercostal muscles contract
      • internal intercostal muscles relax
      • this lifts ribs upwards
      • diaphragm contracts and drops
      • volume of thorax/chest/lungs increases
      • pressure in thorax/chest/lungs decreases
      • air flows in down the pressure gradient
  • Describe how the students could measure their breathing rates.
      • watch how many times the chest rise and fall
      • in one minute
  • Why is water vapor in exhaled air?
      • because water from gas exchange surface evaporates

      • Expired air: 78% nitrogen, 16% O2, 4% CO2, more water vapor

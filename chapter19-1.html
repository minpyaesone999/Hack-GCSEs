Food Chains and Food Webs

  • What is the principal source of energy input to biological systems.
      • Sun
  • Define food chain.
      • transfer of energy from one organism to the next, beginning with a producer
  • Describe how energy is transferred between trophic levels.
      • light energy from Sun - chemical energy in organisms - environment
      • transferred by ingestion
  • Define trophic level.
      • position of an organism in a food chain, food web, pyramid of numbers or a pyramid of biomass
  • Explain why the fourth trophic level has the least biomass in this food chain.
      • energy is lost between the trophic levels
      • not all of the organism is eaten
      • energy is lost in metabolic processes, respiration, and movement
      • energy is lost in excretion
      • so there's less energy to support the next trophic level
  • Arctic wolves are the top carnivores in the food web in the tundra. Explain why eating less meat and more fruits and vegetables is more energy efficient.
      • little energy available from meat - meat comes from primary consumers
      • energy is lost between trophic levels in processes like movement and respiration
      • low numbers of prey (also because energy is lost between trophic levels)
      • wolves may not be successful catching prey

  • Define food web.
      • network of interconnected food chains
  • Define producer.
      • an organism that makes its own organic nutrients
      • usually using energy from sunlight
      • through photosynthesis
  • Define consumer.
      • an organism that gets its energy by feeding on other organisms
  • Define herbivore.
      • animal that gets its energy by eating plants
  • Define carnivore.
      • animal that gets its energy by eating plants
  • Define decomposer.
      • organism that gets its energy from dead or waste organic material

  • Explain the advantages of presenting information about food webs as a pyramid of biomass and not as a pyramid of numbers.

      • biomass indicates how much food there is left

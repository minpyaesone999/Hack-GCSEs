Diffusion

  • Define diffusion.
      • net movement of particles
      • from a region of their higher concentration to a region of their lower concentration
      • down a concentration gradient
      • as a result of their random movement
  • Where does the energy for diffusion come from?
    • the kinetic energy of the random movement of molecules and ions
  • Explain the importance of diffusion of gases and solutes.
      • without it, the molecules needed for life
      • such as oxygen
      • would not be able to get to places they are needed
      • water would always be needed as a solvent
  • Explain how factors like surface area, temperature, concentration gradients, and distance influence diffusion.
      • surface area increase → amount of substances diffusing increase → rate of diffusion increase
      • temperature increase → kinetic energy of particles increase → more movement of particles → rate of diffusion increase
      • concentration gradient increase → more particles will move → rate of diffusion increase
      • distance increase → particles have to travel more distance → rate of diffusion decreases

  • Explain the adaptive features of sperm.
    • flagellum:
      • propels the sperm
      • to site of fertilization (oviduct)
    • mitochondria:
      • aerobic respiration
      • releases energy
    • acrosome:
      • contains enzymes
      • that breaks down jelly coat of egg cells
      • so sperm nucleus can enter the egg cell
  • Explain the adaptive features of egg cells.
    • mitochondria:
      • aerobic respiration
      • releases energy
    • jelly coat:
      • makes sure only one sperm can fertilize the egg
  • Compare male and female gametes in terms of size, structure, motility, and numbers.
      • Size: sperm is 10,000x smaller than egg
      • Structure: (state the differences in adaptive features)
      • Motility: Sperm is motile, egg is not
      • Numbers: Sperm → millions per ejaculation; Egg → 1 per menstrual cycle
  • Describe what happens during fertilization in humans.
      • enzymes from acrosome from sperm break down jelly layer of the egg
      • nucleus from sperm enters egg
      • fertilization membran forms - no more sperm can enter
      • the two halpoid nuclei fuse - zygote is formed
  • Outline the growth and development of the fetus. Describe what happens after fertilization.
      • zygote undergoes mitosis
      • an embryo forms
      • a hollow ball of cells move down oviduct via peristalsis and implants into lining of the uterus (3)
      • placenta develops
      • stem cells form
      • they express different genes to form specialized cells
      • organ systems form
  • Suggest how the embryo is moved along the oviduct.
      • peristalsis - waves of contractions of muscles
      • ciliary action
  • Describe the role of the placenta in humans.
      • gas exchange
      • transfer of dissolved nutrients from mother to fetus by diffusion
      • transfer of excretory products from fetus to mother by diffusion
      • provides passive immunity by transferring antibodies form mother to fetus
      • prevents mixing of blood
  • Describe the role of umbilical cord.
      • contains umbilical artery and umbilical vein
      • umbilical artery: carries deoxygenated blood and waste products from fetus to placenta
      • umbilical vein: carries oxygenated blood and soluble food from placenta to fetus
  • Describe the role of the amniotic sac.
      • membrane enclosing amniotic fluid
      • broken at birth
  • Describe how the fetus obtains glucose.
      • from the mother
      • glucose is carried in blood
      • glucose diffuses from high concentration to low concentration
      • across the placenta through the umbilical cord

  • Why is it recommended that pregnant women do not smoke.
      • toxins in smoke can cross the placenta
      • increased risk of miscarriage or premature birth
      • reduces oxygen available to fetus
      • babies are more likely to be addicted
  • State the functions of amniotic fluid.
      • protects from mechanical shock of fetus
      • maintains constant temperature of fetus
      • allows movement of fetus
      • prevents dehydration
  • Outline the processes involved in labor and birth.
      • breaking of the amniotic sac
      • contraction of the muscles in the uterus wall
      • dilation of the cervix
      • passage through the vagina
      • tying and cutting the umbilical cord
      • delivery of the afterbith
  • Describe the advantages of breast-feeding compared with bottle-feeding.
      • contains antibodies
      • sterile → less risk of infection
      • is at a suitable temperature
      • convenient - always available
      • free
      • nutrient requirements change accordingly with baby's development
  • Describe the disadvantages of breast-feeding compared with bottle-feeding.
      • not all mothers can produce enough milk
      • some drugs can pass through into milk
      • time consuming
      • only mother can produce milk so mother needs to be present
      • can be tiring

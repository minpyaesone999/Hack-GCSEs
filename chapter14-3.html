Hormones in humans

  • Define hormone.
      • chemical substance produced by a gland and carried by the blood
      • which alters the activity of one or more specific target organs
  • Identify specific endocrine glands and their secretions.
      • adrenal glands: adrenaline
      • pancreas: insulin and glucagon
      • testes: testosterone
      • ovaries: oestrogen
  • Outline how adrenaline affects the performance of a swimmer.
      • pulse rate increases
      • breathing rate increases
      • vasodilation in muscle; vasoconstriction in digestive system
      • this diverts blood to muscles away from digestive system
      • increased blood glucose concentration
      • more respiration for more energy for more muscle contraction
      • faster reaction time - pupils widen
      • enhances physical performance
  • Compare nervous and hormonal control systems.
      • effects of hormones are slower than nerves
      • effects are more long-lasting than nerves
      • each hormone may have more than one target organ; nervous system does not have target organ
      • hormones are chemicals transported in blood
      • nerve impulses are electrical coordinated by neurones
  • Explain the advantages of coordinating the response to a dangerous situation using both the nervous system and the endocrine system.
      • nervous system responds immediately
      • nerve impulses travel to specific muscles or effectors to give immediate effect
      • effects of endocrine system are more long lasting
      • hormones travel throughout the body and allow multiple organs to respond to the same signal
      • less energy required than to have nerves going to every cell
      • using both systems stimulates both voluntary and involuntary responses simultaneously
      • more effective than using one system alone
  • Suggest why the blinking of eyes is coordinated by nerves and not hormones.
      • nerves faster/hormones slower
      • nerve impulses are a short lived response

Heart

  • State what is meant by a single circulation.
      • blood travels through the heart once in a complete circuit
  • State what is meant by the term double circulation.
      • one loop to the lungs and one loop to the rest of the body
      • blood flows through the heart twice for one complete circuit
  • Explain the advantages of a double circulation.
      • faster circulation
      • because pressure is higher because blood is pumped twice in one circulation

    • Explain why the wall of the left ventricle is thicker than the wall of the right ventricle.
      • left ventricle contains more muscle
      • because it needs to pump blood further
      • left ventricle needs to overcome more resistance
      • so left ventricle pumps blood at a higher pressure
  • Describe and explain how the blood travels from the right ventricle to the lungs.
      • right ventricle contracts
      • blood pressure increases in heart
      • higher blood pressure in ventricles than in arteries
      • semilunar valve opens
      • blood flows into pulmonary artery
      • semilunar valve closes when blood is in pulmonary artery
  • Explain the importance of the septum.
      • it separates the oxygenated and deoxygenated blood
      • to allow double circulation
  • How is the activity of the heart monitored?
      • pulse rate

      • ECG

Habitat Destruction

  • Describe reasons why people cut down forests.
      • paper
      • wood for building material/furniture/construction
      • firewood
      • to sell wood
      • agriculture
      • urbanization, housing and industry developments
      • extraction of natural resources and minerals
  • Describe the consequences of deforestation on the animals that live in forests. (on forest ecosystem)
      • loss of habitat
      • population decreases
      • extinction of species
      • disruption of food webs
      • loss of biodiversity
      • less food
  • Describe the consequences of deforestation on soil ecosystems.
      • no trees to absorb water → increase in flooding and water logging
      • denitrification increases
      • decrease in desertification
      • soil erosion: roots die → do not bind the soil → soil becomes less intact
      • loss of habit for organisms living in soil
      • disrupts food chian
      • endangers species
  • Explain the risks to a species of having very small numbers.
      • high risk of extinction
      • less chance of reproduction
      • high risk of genetic diseases
      • little to no genetic variation due to reduced number of alleles
      • more vulnerable to diseases or disasters
      • less likely to adapt to change in environment
  • Describe how forests can be conserved.
      • education
      • reforestation
      • government policies and legal quotas
      • management of conflicting demands
      • co-operation with local communities
      • national parks and protected areas

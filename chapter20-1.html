Biotechnology and Genetic Engineering

  • Explain why bacteria are useful in biotechnology and genetic engineering.
      • they are small
      • they reproduce rapidly - reliable and constant supply
      • contain plasmids - you can easily do genetic modification by inserting genes into plasmid
      • no ethical concerns
      • same genetic code as other organisms
      • same DNA
      • can make complex molecules
      • less health risk
  • Explain the advantages of asexual reproduction for insulin production by genetic engineering. (AR: Reproduction)
      • offsprings are all genetically identical so they all contain the desired genes
      • same insulin produced
      • only one cell needs to be engineered
      • rapid
      • large quantity of insulin produced
      • more ethical
  • Name some industrial processes that require yeasts.
      • alcohol production
      • producing biofuel
      • bread making
  • Explain how yeast is used in bread-making.
      • anaerobic respiration
      • carbon dioxide is released
      • bubbles of carbon dioxide causes dough to rise
      • yeast produces enzymes
      • enzymes digest starch
      • ethanol is vaporized
  • A baker made bread at very high temperature. No carbon dioxide was released. State why.
      • enzymes denatured/yeast died
  • Investigate and describe the use of pectinase in fruit juice production.
      • pectin in fruits help plant cell walls stick together
      • pectinase breaks down pectin
      • makes it easier to squeeze juice from fruit
      • fruit juice becomes clearer
  • Investigate and describe the use of biological washing powders that contain enzymes.
      • biological washing powders contain protease, lipase, and carbohydrase to remove stains
      • protease breaks down proteins (i.e blood)
      • lipase break down fats (i.e oil)
      • carbohydrase break carbohydrates-based stains (i.e starch)

  • Define genetic engineering and state examples of where it is used.
      • changing the genetic material of an organism
      • by removing, changing, or inserting individual genes
  • Outline genetic engineering using bacterial production of a human protein as an example.
      • gene that codes for human protein is isolated
      • gene is cut out using restriction enzymes
      • forming sticky ends
      • plasmid is cut using the same restriction enzymes
      • formation of recombinant plasmid
      • ligase is used to join the plasmid and the gene
      • plasmid with the new gene is inserted into bacteria
      • bacteria replicates and produces protein
  • Discuss the advantages of genetically modifying crops such as soya maize and rice.
      • disease resistance
      • larger/faster yield
      • drought resistance/frost resistance
      • salt resistance
      • nutritional enrichment
      • pest resistance
      • herbicide resistance
      • more income
  • Discuss the disadvantages of genetically modifying crops such as soya maize and rice.
      • natural species may die
      • taste is often not as good
      • lead to development of super weeds
      • long term effect on humans unsure

  • Recall Question: Movement in and out of cells
    • from: 0610/43/O/N/18
  • Describe the function of the stomata.
      • allows movement of gases (O2 and CO2) into and out of leaf
      • photosynthesis: CO2 in, O2 out - respiration: O2 in, CO2 out
      • for photosynthesis and respiration
      • it allows transpiration
      • which enables water to be pulled up the plant
      • controls rate of transpiration
  • Explain how the internal structure of a leaf is adapted for photosynthesis.
    • Epidermal cells:
      • Transparent epidermal cells: allow light to pass through
      • Thin epidermal layer (one cell thick): so more light can pass through
    • Palisade mesophyll cells:
      • contain a lot of chloroplasts: so more light energy can be trapped
      • tightly packed: maximizes the light received by cells
      • are located near the top of the leaf: to maximize light received
    • Spongy mesophyll cells:
      • loosely packed: allow the diffusion of gases within the leaf
      • large surface area: cell surfaces are sites of gas exchange
  • Explain why spongy mesophyll cells should have large surface area.
      • faster rate of diffusion
      • carbon dioxide is raw material needed for photosynthesis
      • more absorption of carbon dioxide
      • more absorption of oxygen for aerobic respiration
      • more evaporation of water to increase transpirational pull
  • Explain why there are many interconnecting air spaces within the leaf.
      • air spaces connect to outside air via stomata
      • allows for the diffusion of gases throughout the whole of the leaf
      • faster diffusion
      • allows more efficient photosynthesis, respiration, and evaporation for transpiration

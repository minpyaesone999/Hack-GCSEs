Variation and Sickle-Cell Anaemia

  • Define variation.
      • differences between individuals of the same species
  • Distinguish between phenotypic variation and genetic variation.
      • phenotypic variation is caused by both genetic and environmental factors
      • genetic variation is caused only by genetic factors
  • How can you tell if something is an example of discontinuous variation.
      • there are distinct phenotypes
      • no continuous range
      • controlled by genes
      • not affected by the environment
  • How can you tell if something is an example of continuous variation
      • phenotypes have intermediates
      • continuous range
      • affected by both genes and environment

  • Describe some symptoms of anaemia.
      • tired
      • shortness of breath
      • headache
      • chest pain
      • fast heartbeat
  • Explain how red blood cells become sickle-shaped. Describe the cause of sickle-cell anaemia.
      • caused by a mutation
      • change in DNA/base sequence
      • of gene for haemoglobin
      • causes a different sequence of amino acids
      • abnormal haemoglobin produced
      • red blood cells become sickle-shaped
      • allele for sickle cell anaemia is inherited
  • Explain why sickle-cell anaemia is a disease that reduces the delivery of oxygen to tissues.
      • haemoglobin is abnormal
      • abnormal haemoglobin carries less oxygen
      • red blood cells become sickle-shaped
      • sickle cells stick together and clot in blood vessels
      • fewer red blood cells
  • Some people who have sickle-cell anaemia have parents who do not have sickle-cell anaemia. Explain how people with sickle cell anaemia inherit the disease. (AR Inheritance)
      • both parents carry the allele for sickle cell anaemia
      • both parents are heterozygous
      • half of the gametes of both parents have the recessive allele.
      • children who are homozygous recessive have sickle cell anaemia
      • there is a 0.25 chance of being homozygous recessive
  • Explain why the distribution of sickle cell anaemia and malaria are similar.
      • malaria is a severe disease (may be fatal)
      • it acts as a selective agent
      • people with sickle cell anaemia allele are resistant to malaria
      • homozygous dominant (homozygous normal) are susceptible to malaria
      • they are more likely to die of malaria before having children and passing on the genes
      • homozygous recessive (homozygous sickle cell) die from sickle cell anaemia
      • sickle cell carriers (heterozygous) do not die from sickle cell anaemia
      • only sickle cell carriers have children and pass on genes and pass on the sickle cell allele as well
      • therefore, sickle cell anaemia gene keeps on being passed on in regions of malaria

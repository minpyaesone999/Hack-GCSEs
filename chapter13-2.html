Ultrafiltration and Selective Reabsorption

  • Describe the structure of a glomerulus.
      • network of tangled capillaries
      • round (description of shape)
      • capillaries are one cell thick
      • capillaries have pores that are smaller than protein molecules and cells so they can't pass through
  • Describe the role of the glomerulus/renal capsule.
      • ultrafiltration
      • it contains blood at high pressure: so molecules are forced out
      • provides a large surface area
      • small or soluble particles that are filtered out e.g. glucose, urea, water
      • large or insoluble molecules stay in glomerulus e.g. proteins, blood cells
  • Which waste substances are removed from the blood.
      • urea
      • uric acid
      • ammonia
      • toxins
      • salt/ions
      • water
  • How much protein would you expect in the urine of a healthy person.
      • 0. proteins are too big to pass through the capillary wall in the glomerulus.
  • What are the three factors that affect the volume and concentration of urine?
      • water intake
      • temperature
      • exercise
  • Describe how salts are reabsorbed against a concentration gradient. Active Recall: Movement in and out of cells
      • carrier proteins in cell membranes are complementary to salts
      • salts enter protein channels
      • the carrier proteins move ions from low concentration to high concentration by changing shape
      • this process uses energy from respiration
      • this is active transport
  • Explain how the cells that line the kidney tubules absorb many compounds from the filtrate.
      • cells contain microvilli
      • microvilli give a large surface area
      • for more efficient diffusion
      • lots of mitochondria
      • mitochondria are the site of aerobic respiration and they provide energy
      • energy is needed for active transport
      • active transport is needed to absorb compounds against the concentration gradient

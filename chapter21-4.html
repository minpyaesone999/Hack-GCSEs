Conservation

  • Define a sustainable resource.
      • one which is produced as rapidly as it is removed from the environment
      • so it does not run out
  • Define sustainable development.
      • development providing for the needs of an increasing human population
      • without harming the environment
  • Explain what sustainable development requires.
      • management of conflicting demands
      • planning and co-operation at local, national and international levels
  • Outline how sewage is treated to make the water that it contains safe to return to the environment or for human use.
      • polluted water is piped to sewage treatment
      • screening → removal of large pieces of waste
      • coagulation to separate suspended particles
      • settling of grit in the grit settlement tanks
      • digestion by bacteria
      • with aeration tank
      • sludge treated with anaerobic decomposers → anaerobic digestion
      • water treated with chlorine
      • distillation/collection of water through evaporation
  • Explain the roles of bacteria in the aerobic digestion tank in sewage treatment.
      • secrete enzymes
      • breakdown insoluble substances to soluble substances
      • (give examples) protease breaks down protein to amino acids, etc.
  • Describe the negative consequences of letting untreated sewage flow into lake ecosystems.
      • eutrophication
      • aquatic plants die → cannot photosynthesize due to blocked light
      • decreased pH kills aquatic organisms
      • oxygen concentration decreases due to bacteria and decomposers → populations of animals decrease
      • disrupts food chains
      • more mosquitoes
      • toxicity and mutations caused by heavy metals
      • female contraceptive hormones cause feminization of aquatic organisms and reduced sperm count
  • Outline how paper can be recycled.
      • paper is collected and sent to recycling center
      • shredding
      • pulping
      • soaking with water
      • de-inking
      • bleached
      • rolled, and flattened
  • Outline the possible benefits of conserving a habitat.
      • prevents extinction
      • maintains genetic diversity
      • maintaining habitat
      • nutrient recycling
      • maintain food webs
      • prevent soil erosion
  • State some resources which natural ecosystems provide for humans.
      • food
      • medicine
      • fuel
      • water, oxygen
      • mineral
  • Describe ways in which endangered species can be conserved.
      • reducing pollution
      • monitor populations
      • protect habitat
      • removal of alien species
      • prevent hunting
      • create reserves and exclusion zones
      • international agreements to limit trade
      • captive breeding
  • Suggest why replanted forests and plantations are less useful for conservation than the natural forest.
      • planted forest is most likely a monoculture
      • so there is a loss of biodiversity
      • (negative effects of monoculture)

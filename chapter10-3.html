Passive Immunity

  • Define passive immunity and how it is gained.
      • short-term defense against pathogens
      • no memory cells or antibodies produced by the body
      • from antibodies acquired elsewhere
      • provide example e.g. breast-feeding/antitoxin/antivenom/tetanus injection
  • Explain why passive immunity does not give long-term protection against diseases such as meningitis.
      • there is no active immune response
      • antibodies are not made by the body's own lymphocytes
      • no memory cells are created
      • antibodies are eventually broken down in the body
  • Explain the differences between active and passive immunity.
      • long term vs. short term
      • active immunity has slower response than passive immunity
      • active immunity produces memory cells → passive immunity doesn't
      • antibodies are produced by the body in active immunity → antibodies are acquired from another individual in passive immunity
  • Explain why it is important for newborn babies to have antibodies.
      • it provides passive immunity
      • proteins against infection
      • gives short-term immunity to diseases that mother has had
      • important because immune system of babies are not yet developed

Cell structure and organisation

  • Describe and compare the structures of a plant cell with an animal cell.
      • Animal cells have nucleus, cell membrane, cytoplasm
      • Plant cells have nucleus, cell membrane, cytoplasm, cell wall, large permanent vacuole, chloroplasts (most, mainly ones in leaf)
    • from: 0610/41/M/J/19
  • State the functions of the cell wall.
    • gives the cell its shape
    • protects it from mechanical damage and provides strength to cell
  • State the functions of the nucleus.
    • controls activities in the cell
    • contains DNA
    • controls how cells develop
  • State the function of the cytoplasm.
    • many chemical reactions occur in the cytoplasm
  • State the function of chloroplasts.
    • site of photosynthesis in plants
  • State the function of vacuoles.
    • stores water, amino acids, and sugars
    • control turgor pressure within cells
  • State the functions of the cell membrane.
    • controls movement in and out of the cell
    • creates barrier between cell and its surroundings
  • State the functions of the mitochondria.
    • aerobic respiration
    • release energy
  • Suggest why the inner membrane of mitochondria is folded.
    • to provide large surface area
    • so there's more surface for respiration
    • allows faster/more efficient respiration
  • Explain why cells would die if the mitochondria doesn't function.
    • no respiration
    • cannot release energy

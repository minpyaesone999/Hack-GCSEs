  • Describe what happens to carbohydrates in the human body between ingestion and assimilation in the liver.
      • ingestion of carbohydrates
      • mechanical digestion in mouth breaks carbohydrates into physically smaller pieces
      • moves through oesophagus - churned in the stomach (mechanical digestion)
      • bile neutralizes stomach acid to provide a pH of 7 /8 which is optimum for amylase and maltase
      • amylase is secreted by pancreas and amylase breaks insoluble starch to soluble maltose in duodenum (also happened in mouth when salivary glands secrete amylase)
      • this is chemical digestion
      • maltase is on epithelium of small intestine
      • maltase breaks down maltose to glucose
      • absorption into blood by diffusion and active transport through villi into capillaries
      • microvilli increases surface area for absorption
  • Describe what happens to proteins in the human body between ingestion and assimilation in the liver.
      • ingestion of proteins
      • mechanical digestion in mouth breaks proteins into physically smaller pieces
      • moves through oesophagus - churned in the stomach (mechanical digestion)
      • pepsin is secreted in stomach and pepsin breaks down proteins to polypeptides in stomach (optimum pH of pepsin is pH 2)
      • this is chemical digestion
      • bile neutralizes stomach acid to provide a pH of 7 /8 which is optimum for trypsin
      • trypsin breaks down polypeptides to amino acids in small intestine
      • absorption into blood by diffusion and active transport through villi into capillaries
      • microvilli increases surface area for absorption
  • Describe what happens to fats in the human body between ingestion and assimilation in the liver.
      • ingestion of fats
      • mechanical digestion in mouth breaks fats into physically smaller pieces
      • moves through oesophagus - churned in the stomach (mechanical digestion)
      • bile emulsifies fats to increase the surface area for the chemical digestion of fat to fatty acids and glycerol
      • bile neutralizes stomach acid to provide a pH of 7 /8 which is optimum for lipase
      • lipase breaks down fat to fatty acids and glycerol
      • absorption into lacteals
      • microvilli increases surface area for absorption
      • travel via lymph vessles throughout body until lymph empties into blood
  • Describe the role of bile in digestion.
      • emulsification
      • increased surface area of fat globules
      • faster break down of fats by
      • lipase to fatty acids and glycerol
      • neutralizes stomach acid

Adaptive Features

  • Define fitness.
      • the probability of an organism surviving and reproducing in the environment in which it is found
  • Define adaptive feature.
      • inherited functional features of an organism
      • that increase its fitness
  • State some adaptations of the leaf for survival in a dry environment.
      • sunken stomata
      • hairs
      • fleshy leaves
      • thick cuticle
      • fewer shedding of leaves
  • Explain the adaptive features of hydrophytes to their environments.
      • few roots - no need for roots to absorb water
      • leaves have large surface area - to float
      • no cuticle - no need to conserve water
      • stomata only on upper surface - only upper surface exposed to air
      • lots of air spaces between cells - for buoyancy
  • Explain the adaptive features of xerophytes to their environments.
      • deep roots - reach water far underground
      • needele-like leaves - minimum surface area to minimize transpiration
      • shallow spreading roots - collect occasioal rainwater
      • (can include adaptive features of leaves)

Selection

  • Describe what evolution is.
      • the change in adaptive features of a population over time
      • as the result of natural selection
  • Explain how strains of antibiotic-resistant bacteria are formed.
      • antibiotic resistant bacteria are formed by mutation
      • there was a change in their DNA
      • which results in different proteins produced
      • which enable resistance
  • Explain how strains of antibiotic-resistant bacteria are spread.
      • when antibiotic is used, susceptible bacteria die
      • there's less competition for antibiotic resistant bacteria
      • the resistant bacteria is able to reproduce faster (due to fewer limiting factors)
      • they pass on their gene for antibiotic resistance
      • they are transmitted from host to host through direct contact (e.g. sexual intercourse) or indirectly (e.g. from the air

  • Explain the significance of meiosis on an endangered species. Explain why variation is an advantage to a species.
      • meiosis causes genetic variation
      • enables endangered species to adapt better to new environment
      • allows natural selection
      • better suited organisms reproduce
      • this allows evolution
      • eventually reduces competition since they are better adapted for survival
  • Describe the process of natural selection.
      • variation exists within population due to meiosis
      • there are many offsprings produced
      • competition for resources and struggle for survival
      • best adapted organisms are most likely to survive
      • these individuals that are better adapted to these environment reproduce - the others die
      • adaptive alleles are passed on to the next generation
      • this leads to evolution
  • Describe the process of selective breeding.
      • parents with desirable features are selected
      • they are crossed together
      • select the offsprings with desirable features
      • crossed these offsprings together
      • continue this selective breeding process over many generations
  • Describe how growers selectively breed plants.
      • cross parent plants with desired features
      • by transferring pollen with a brush
      • choose offsprings with desired features and cross them with each other
      • keep crossing for many generations
  • Explain the difference between natural selection and selective breeding.
      • the favorable features in natural selection are adaptive features
      • natural selection is caused by the environment
      • natural selection is slower
      • natural selection results in an increase in fitness
      • natural selection maintains genetic variation
      • natural selection has random mating

Methods of Birth Control in Humans

  • Outline the four methods of birth control.
      • natural: abstinence, monitoring body temperature, cervical mucus
      • chemical: IUD, IUS, contraceptive pill, hormone injection
      • barrier: condom, femidom, diaphragm
      • surgical: vasectomy, female sterilization
  • Outline the use of hormones in fertility treatments.
      • FSH and LH
      • FSH stimulates the production of follicles and maturation of eggs
      • more eggs are relased
      • LH stimulates ovulation
  • Outline the use of hormones in contraceptive treatments.
      • oestrogen and progesterone
      • oestrogen causes FSH concentration to decrease
      • follicle stops forming and egg cells don't mature
      • progesterone causes LH concentration to decrease
      • egg cell is not relased
  • Outline artificial insemination.
      • collect sperm from male
      • place sperm into uterus
      • around the time of ovulation
  • Outline in vitro fertilization.
      • egg cell is collected from ovary
      • sperm cells are collected from male
      • they are placed in petri dish
      • fertilization occurs - zygote forms - embryo forms
      • embryo is placed back inside female's uterus
  • Outline the social implications of using fertility drugs/IVF.
      • religious/ethical objections to use of fertility drugs
      • treatment could be costly and lead to financial problems within family
      • multiple births (twins, triplets) can occur and they might be unwanted
      • stress is usually associated with difficulty having children
    • Exclusively for IVF:
      • allows parents of same sex to have children
  • Outline the social implications of contraceptive treatments.
      • increase in educated women
      • increase in employed women

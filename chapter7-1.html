Diet

  • What is meant by the term balanced diet?
      • has all essential nutrients
      • carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins, minerals, fibre
      • in correct proportions
      • to maintain good health
  • Explain how age, gender and activity affect the dietary needs of humans including during pregnancy and whilst breast-feeding.
      • Children: more calcium for better bone growth
      • Teenagers: high calorie intake
      • Males generally require more energy
      • Females need more iron because blood is lost through menstruation
      • Pregnant: more iron, calcium, other healthy nutrients
  • Describe the effects of malnutrition.
      • weight loss, starvation
      • deficiency disease
      • iron deficiency → anaemia
      • protein deficiency → kwashiorkor
      • deficiency of all nutrients → marasmus
      • vitamin C deficiency → scurvy
      • fibre deficiency → constipation
      • too much fat → obesity, coronary heart disease
  • List the principal sources of and describe the dietary importance of carbohydrates, fats, proteins, vitamin C, vitamin D, calcium, iron, fibre, and water.
  • Describe the symptoms of marasmus
      • lack of growth
      • diarrhea
      • fatigue
      • more prone to infections
  • Describe the symptoms of kwashiorkor.
      • rounded belly
      • oedema → exces of liquid in tissue fluid

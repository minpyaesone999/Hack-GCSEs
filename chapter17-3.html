Meiosis

  • Define meiosis.
      • reduction division in which the chromosome number is halved from diploid to haploid
      • resulting in genetically different cells
  • Explain how meiosis can result in variation in a species.
      • meiosis produces gametes
      • gametes are haploid
      • gametes are genetically different from each other
      • gametes are fused at random
      • zygotes/offspring are genetically different from parents
      • sexual reproduction causes variation
  • Why is it important for gametes to be haploid?
      • so that the chromosome number does not double at fertilziation
      • so that the chromosome number remains constant from generation to generation
  • Explain why meiosis is necessary in the testes.
      • to produce sperm
      • for sexual reproduction
      • to produce haploid cells
      • so that when fertilization occurs, the number of chromosomes return to the diploid number
      • creates genetic variation

Osmosis

  • Define osmosis.
      • net movement of water molecules
      • from a region of higher water potential (diluted solution) to a region of lower water potential (concentrated solution)
      • through a partially permeable membrane
  • Explain the effects on plant tissues of immersing them in a concentrated salt solution.
      • salt solution has lower water potential than the cell
      • water moves out of cell via osmosis
      • down the water potential gradient
      • through a partially permeable membrane
      • vacuole decreases in size
      • some parts of cell membrane pulls away form cell wall
      • cells are plasmolysed
      • cells become flaccid because they lose turgor pressure
      • cell walls no longer withstand pressure
  • Explain the effects on plant tissues of immersing them in a diluted salt solution.
      • salt solution has higher water potential than the cell
      • water enters into cell via osmosis
      • down the water potential gradient
      • through a partially permeable membrane
      • vacuole increases in size
      • vacuole pushes outwards onto cell membrane
      • turgor pressure inside cell increases
      • cell becomes turgid

  • Explain the importance of water potential and osmosis in the uptake of water by plants.
      • plants uptake water via osmosis
      • so water potential gradient needs to be maintained
      • so water can travel from a region of higher water potential (soil) to a region of lower water potential (cell)
  • Explain how plants are supported.
      • they are supported by the turgor pressure within cells
      • which is the water pressure acting against an inelastic cell wall
      • this gives the plant cells structure and holds the plant intact

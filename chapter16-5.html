Sex Hormones in Humans

  • Which hormones are responsible for the development and regulation of secondary sexual characteristics during puberty.
      • male - testosterone
      • female - oestrogen
  • Describe the whole menstrual cycle and the roles of the hormones FSH, LH, progesterone, and oestrogen.
      • pituitary gland produces FSH
      • follicle starts forming
      • egg cell matures inside
      • follicle produces oestrogen
      • oestrogen causes FSH concentration to decrease
      • uterus lining thickens
      • pituitary gland produces LH
      • egg cell is released from follicle into oviduct (ovulation)
      • follicle forms into corpus luteum
      • corpus luteum produces progresterone
      • progesterone maintains uterus lining
      • progesterone causes LH concentration to decrease
      • corpus luteum dies
      • progesterone level decreases
      • lining breaks
      • menstruation
  • State two places where progesterone is produced during pregnancy.
      • corpus luteum
      • placenta
  • Describe the role of progesterone in the menstrual cycle.
      • maintains uterine lining
      • inhibits FSH/ LH (release)

Photosynthesis

  • Define photosynthesis.
    • process by which plants manufacture carbohydrates
    • from raw materials
    • using energy from light
  • State the word and the balanced equations for photosynthesis.
    • carbon dioxide + water â†’ glucose + oxygen
  • Explain the function of chlorophyll.
    • to transfer light energy into chemical energy in molecules
    • for the synthesis of carbohydrates
  • Explain why plants take up carbon dioxide during photosynthesis.
    • carbon dioxide is raw material for photosynthesis
  • Describe the process of photosynthesis to describe how glucose is produced in leaves.
      • CO2 enters leaf
      • CO2 diffuses to cells
      • chlorophyll traps light energy
      • light energy is used to make glucose and oxygen (as by-product) using CO2 and water as raw materials
      • so light energy is converted into chemical energy
      • (include balanced chemical equation)
  • Explain how a molecule of carbon dioxide from the atmosphere reaches the site of photosynthesis in a leaf. Describe its path.
      • higher concentration of CO2 outside leaf than inside leaf
      • diffuses down concentration gradient
      • through stomata and through intercellular air spaces and through cell wall, cell membrane and cytoplasm
      • into chloroplast of palisade mesophyll cell
      • reacts with water to form glucose
  • Explain why oxygen passes out of the leaf during the daytime.
      • oxygen is a by-product of photosynthesis
      • more oxygen is produced than used in respiration
      • because in daytime, rate of photosynthesis is greater than rate of respirationmore oxygen is produced than used in respiration
      • concentration inside the leaf is greater than outside
      • oxygen diffuses down its concentration gradient

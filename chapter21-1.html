Food Supply

  • Describe how modern technology has resulted in increased food production.
      • agricultural machinery to use larger areas of land an improve efficiency
      • chemical fertilizers to improve yield
      • insecticides to improve quality and yield
      • herbicides to reduce competition with weeds
      • selective breeding to improve production by crop plants and livestock
  • Explain the benefits of killing pests.
      • reduced damage to crops
      • increased yield and quality of crops
      • more income
      • because more sugar will be available for growth
      • reduced disease transmission
  • Describe the negative impacts to an ecosystem of large-scale monocultures of crop plants.
      • if there's a natural disaster/infectious disease → all crops gone
      • low biodiversity → not support for each other
      • increase pests - harm crops from viruses
      • insecticides easily give resistance
  • Describe the negative impacts to an ecosystem of intensive livestock production.
      • easy spread of diseases
      • welfare issues for livestock
      • waste from livestock production pollute land/waterways nearby
  • Explain the environmental damage that may be caused by herbicides.
      • kills other plants that are not weeds
      • harms insect pollinators and animals
      • loss of biodiversity
      • can run off into streams
      • selective pressure to weeds → weeds develop resistance → become more difficult to control
  • Discuss the problems which contribute to famine.
      • drought → lack of water for irrigation and to water plants
      • flooding
      • fire
      • other natural disasters (name them: like tornadoes, earthquakes, etc.)
      • war
      • rising prices of food
      • poverty and unequal distribution of food
      • soil degradation

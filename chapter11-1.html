  • Describe the function of cartilage in the gas exchange system and its structure.
      • it's horse-shoe shaped and open at posterior end
      • keeps the trachea open - prevents collapsion
      • allows the free flow of air into lungs
      • allows us to breathe even when bent or swallowing
  • State some features of gas exchange surfaces.
      • large surface area - increases rate of diffusion
      • thin surface - short diffusion distance
      • many capillaries - good blood supply
      • good ventilation
      • permeable
      • moist
  • Suggest why the alveoli have thin walls.
      • for gas exchange
      • short distance for diffusion
      • fast gas exchange
  • Describe how oxygen molecules move from the alveoli into the blood.
      • diffusion
      • across cell membranes
      • high concentration to low concentration of oxygen
      • moist lining to dissolve oxygen
  • What are the consequences of humidity dropping on gas exchange systems?
      • evaporation increases
      • no more moist surface
      • gas exchange rate decreases

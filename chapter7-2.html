Alimentary Canal

  • Define ingestion.
      • taking of substances into the body through the mouth
  • Define mechanical digestion.
      • breakdown of food into smaller pieces
      • without chemical change to the food molecules
  • Define chemical digestion.
      • breakdown of large, insoluble molecules
      • into small, soluble molecules
  • Define absorption.
      • movement of small food molecules and ions
      • through the wall of the intestine into the blood
  • Define assimilation.
      • movement of digested food molecules
      • into the cells of the body
      • where they are used, becoming part of the cells
  • Define egestion.
      • passing out of food that has not be digested or absorbed
      • as faeces, through the anus
  • Define diarrhea.
      • loss of watery faeces
  • Describe the effects of diarrhea on the body and your health.
      • watery faeces
      • dehydration
      • loss of ions
      • stomach pain
  • Describe and explain the effects of the cholera bacteria on the gut.
      • produces a toxin
      • toxin attaches to the wall of the small intestine
      • secretion of chloride ions into the small intestine
      • causing a water potential gradient - water potential of the intestinal lumen is lowered
      • causes the osmotic movement of water into the gut, water flows from the cells/blood into the lumen
      • loss of salts from the blood
      • causes diarrhea
  • Outline the treatment of diarrheo.
      • oral rehydration solution
      • the salt reduces water potential gradient in gut
      • replenishes energy for active transport
      • the water rehydrates you (because you lost a lot of water

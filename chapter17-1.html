Chromosomes, Genes, and Proteins

  • Define inheritance.
      • the transmission of genetic information from generation to generation
  • Define chromosome.
      • thread-like structure of DNA
      • carrying genetic information
      • in the form of genes
  • Define gene.
      • length of DNA that codes for a protein
  • Define allele.
      • version of a gene
  • Define haploid nucleus.
      • nucleus containing a single set of unpaired chromosomes e.g. in gametes
      • in human → 23 chromosomes
  • Define diploid nucleus.
      • nucleus containing a single set of unpaired chromosomes e.g. in gametes
      • in human → 46 chromosomes
  • Explain how proteins are made in a cell.
      • the gene coding for the protein remains in the nucleus
      • mRNA molecules carry a copy of the gene to the cytoplasm
      • the mRNA passes through ribosomes
      • the ribosome assembles amino acids into protein molecules
      • the specific order of amino acids is determined by the sequence of bases in the mRNA
  • Describe the differences between a storage protein and an enzyme.
      • different sequences of amino acids
      • composed of different amino acids
      • they have different shapes
  • Explain how DNA controls cell function.
      • by controlling the production of proteins, which are enzymes, antibodies and receptors for neurotransmitters

Enzyme Basics

  • Define catalyst.
    • a substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction
    • and is not changed by the reaction
  • Define enzymes.
    • proteins that function as biological catalysts
  • Why are enzymes important in all living organisms?
    • they increase the rate of chemical reactions to a suitable rate that is necessary to sustain life
  • State the product of the action of protease on the protein fibrin.
    • amino acid

  • Describe in detail how enzymes function.
      • enzymes are proteins
      • they are unchanged in a reaction
      • enzymes are specific
      • they speed up reaction by lowering activation energy
      • substrates fit to the active sites of their complementary enzyme
      • like a lock and key
      • to form enzyme-substrate complex
      • product molecules are released
  • Explain why the shape of an enzyme is important.
      • active site of enzyme must be complementary in shape to the substrates
      • like a lock and key
      • to make enzyme-substrate complex
      • so enzymes are specific to one substrate

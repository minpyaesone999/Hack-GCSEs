Sense Organs

  • Define sense organ.
      • groups of receptor cells responding to specific stimuli: light, sound, touch, temperature and chemicals
  • Past-Paper Question 1
    • from: 0610/41/O/N/18
  • State the name of the process that allows the eye to view objects at different distances.
      • accommodation
  • Outline the pathway in a reflex arc in response to shining a bright light into the eye.
      • stimulus is detected by the retina (receptor)
      • electrical impulse is created
      • sensory neurone → relay neurone → motor neurone
      • reference to synapses between neurones
      • effector is the circular muscles in iris which contract as a response
  • Explain the pupil reflex in terms of light intensity and antagonistic action of circular and radial muscles in the iris. Explain in terms of the reflex arc.
  • Explain the accommodation to view near and distant objects. Explain in terms of the reflex arc.
  • Describe the role of rod cells.
      • sensitive to light in low-intensity (2)
      • passes impulse to optic nerve
  • Describe the role of cone cells.
      • cone cells detect color
      • there are three kinds of cones (red, green, blue)
      • which absorb light of different colors for color vision
  • Explain why the eye cannot easily identify different colors in low levels of light.
      • cones are less sensitive in low light
      • cones detect color
      • rods work in low light but can't detect color
  • Describe and explain the distribution of rod cells and cone cells across the retina.
      • maximum number of cone cells are at the fovea
      • maximum number of rod cells at the sides
      • only cone cells at the fovea/no rod cells
      • no rod cells and no cone cells at blind spot
      • there is uneven distribution of rod cells at either side of the fovea
      • more rod cells than cone cells overall in the retina

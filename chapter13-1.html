Excretion

  • Describe how excess amino acids are broken down.
      • process is called deamination: removal of nitrogen containing part of excess amino acids to form urea
      • part of amino acid is converted to ammonia
      • ammonia converted to urea
  • Describe the functions of the liver in the body.
      • produces bile salts from the break-down products of haemoglobin
      • deamination
      • stores glycogen and converts glycogen to glucose and vice versa when necessary
      • makes plasma proteins
  • Explain the role of the kidney in excretion.
      • filters blood
      • filtration occurs in the glomerulus
      • reabsorption of useful substances later in the tubules
      • excretes nitrogenous waste e.g. urea
      • excess mineral salts
      • excess water
  • How is carbon dioxide excreted? Explain why this process is termed excretion.
      • CO2 is excreted through the lungs
      • CO2 is metabolic waste
      • it is also toxic

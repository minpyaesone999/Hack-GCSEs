Blood and Tissue Fluid

  • Identify the different types of blood cells. and state their functions.
    • Red Blood Cells
      • transports oxygen
      • haemoglobin binds to oxygen to form oxyhaemoglobin
    • White Blood Cells
      • defends body from pathogens
      • phagocytes: phagocytosis
      • lymphocytes: antibody production
    • Platelets: clotting
    • Plasma: transport blood cells, ions, soluble nutrients (glucose, amino acids, etc.) , hormones, carbon dioxide

  • Describe how substances move from the blood in the capillaries into the tissue fluid.
      • oxygen diffuses from blood to tissue fluid
      • across the wall of capillary
      • down a concentration gradient
      • the pressure in capillary forces out nutrient solutes dissovled in water
      • large insoluble molecules too large to pass through capillary pores
  • Describe how a blood clot forms.
      • platelets activate
      • the conversion of fibrinogen to fibrin
      • soluble to insoluble (fibrinogen is soluble, fibrin is insoluble)
      • fibrin forms a mesh
      • traps red blood cells
  • State the importance of blood clotting.
      • prevents blood loss
      • prevent pathogens from entering a wound

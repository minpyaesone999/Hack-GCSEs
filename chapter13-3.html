Diabetes and Dialysis

  • Why is the patient with Type 1 diabetes urinating often?
      • glucose passed through to tubule
      • reabsorption capacity reduces
      • glucose draws water
  • Explain dialysis and how it works.
      • blood passes over a dialysis membrane
      • dialysis membrane separates the person's blood and the dialysis fluid
      • dialysis fluid contains glucose/salts/no urea
      • urea, excess salt, water enters dialysis fluid by diffusion
      • dialysis fluid is refreshed
      • glucose in dialysis fluid should be same concentration as should be in blood so there's no net loss of glucose

  • Before a kidney is transplanted, it is important to match the tissue type of the donor with the tissue type of the recipient. State why this is necessary.
      • to avoid rejection
      • to stop immune system from attacking the new kidney
  • Discuss the advantages and disadvantages of kidney transplants, compared with dialysis.
    • Advantages of having kidney transplant instead of dialysis:
      • convenience: no need to visit hospital, spend time with dialysis, have a restricted diet - it's a one-time treatment
      • improved quality of life - can eat normally again
      • cost effective in the long run
      • you still have a working kidney
    • Disadvantages:
      • rejection of kidney is possible
      • risk associated with operation
      • difficult to find a suitable donor
      • need to take immunosuppressant drugs
  • Explain why urine tests are a good indicator of how much salt has been consumed.
      • excess salt is excreted from body in urine
      • some salt is reabsorbed in the kidney tubules into the blood
      • people are not reliable in recording how much salt they have taken

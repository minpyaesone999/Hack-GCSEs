Transpiration

  • Define transpiration.
      • loss of water vapor from plant leaves
      • by evaporation of water at the surfaces of the mesophyll cells
      • followed by diffusion of water vapor through the stomata

    • from: 0610/33/O/N/15
  • Explain the importance of increasing humidity near the leaf surface.
      • increased reduce humidity will reduce transpiration rate
      • reduced water potential gradient in plant
      • causes less water loss
      • reduces changes of wilting
  • State and explain the effect of an increase in temperature on the rate of transpiration.
      • kinetic energy of molecules of water increases
      • increases rate of diffusion of water vapor
      • more evaporation
      • increase in rate of transpiration
      • stomatal pores become wider/narrower
  • Explain how the internal structure of leaves results in the loss of large quantities of water in transpiration.
      • xylem supplies water
      • water evaporates from surface of mesophyll cells
      • air spaces allow evaporation of water
      • large internal surface area increases evaporation rate
      • guard cells, open (or) close stomata
      • water vapor diffuses out through stomata

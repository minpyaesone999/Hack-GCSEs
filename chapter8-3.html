Wilting

  • Recall Question: Movement in and out of cells
    • from: 0610/42/O/N/19
  • Explain how and why wilting occurs.
      • conditions: low amounts of water, high temperature, low humidity, etc. (anything that causes a lack of water)
      • water travels out of cells via osmosis
      • down the water potential gradient
      • turgor pressure of water on inelastic cell wall decreases
      • plant cell becomes flaccid
      • water in cells are not being replaced as quickly as they are lost
      • cells rely on turgor pressure for structural support to prevent wilting
  • Suggest the advantages to a plant of wilting.
      • stomata closes
      • prevents more water loss
      • water conserved for other parts of the plant
      • decreased surface area that is exposed to the Sun â†’ lower absorption of heat so lower water loss

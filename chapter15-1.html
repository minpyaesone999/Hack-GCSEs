Medicinal Drugs

  • Define drug.
      • any substance taken into the body that modifies or affects chemical reactions in the body
  • Explain why nicotine is a drug.
      • it modifies chemical reactions in the body
  • Explain how development of resistant bacteria such as MRSA can be minimised.
      • use antibiotics only when essential
      • ensure that treatment is completed

  • Explain why it is so important to complete a full treatment of antibiotics.
      • all bacteria need to be killed
      • any remaining bacteria will reproduce
      • disease will continue
      • bacteria may develop resistance to the antibiotics
      • antibiotics are no longer effective
      • new antibiotics have to be given
      • new antibiotics - more selection pressure - more resistance
  • Explain why antibiotics kill bacteria, but do not affect viruses.
      • antibiotics target peptidoglycan cell wall
      • viruses do not have cell walls, bacteria have cell walls

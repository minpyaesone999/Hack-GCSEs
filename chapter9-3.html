Blood and Lymphatic Vessels

  • Past-Paper Question 1
    • from: 0610/42/O/N/17
  • Explain how the structures of arteries are adapted for their functions.
      • thick wall: withstands high blood pressure
      • elastic tissue: recoils to maintain blood pressure
      • small lumen: maintains blood pressure
  • Explain how the structures of veins are adapted for their functions.
      • contains valves: prevent backflow
      • large lume: low resistance to blood flow
      • thinner walls than arteries: allows vein to be squeezed by surrounding msucles
  • Explain how the structures of capillaries are adapted for their functions.
      • very thin wall: for efficient diffusion of gases in gas exchange
      • small diameter: so many capillaries can fit into tissues so capillaries can reach every cell throughout the body
      • have pores: allow substances to pass in and out of blood easily
      • extensive network: large surface area for diffusion
  • State the functions of arterioles, venules, and shunt vessels.
      • regulate blood flow into tissue
      • by increasing or decreasing their diameters
      • arterioles control blood flow to capillaries
      • venules control outflow of blood
      • shunt vessels link arteries to veins
      • allows blood to bypass capillaries
  • Explain why blood pressure must decrease in the arterioles before entering the capillaries.
      • high pressure would burst capillaries
      • because capillary walls are thin
      • they are only one cell thick
  • Outline the functions of a lymphatic system.
      • tissue fluids drains into lymphatic system
      • lymph vessels transport tissue fluids
      • back into the circulatory system
      • contains lymphocytes in lymph nodes
      • defence against pathogens and provide active immunity
      • lacteals absorb and transport fats
      • from small intestine
